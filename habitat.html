Habitat of The Royal Bengal Tiger

The famous Royal Bengal Tiger is currently distributed along India, Bangladesh, Nepal, Bhutan and China. Although its historical range used to spam from Myanmar in the East to the Indus Basin
(modern day Pakistan) in the West, it has since gone extinct in those areas and remains present only in the above mentioned areas currently.

The current distribution of the Bengal Tiger

India

India is the country with the highest population of Bengal Tigers, with an estimated population of about 3500. Madhya Pradesh, Uttarakhand, Karnataka, Tamil Nadu and West Bengal are some of
the states with the highest tiger population in India. Some famous spots where the Bengal Tiger resides are :

  • Himalayas (Shivaliks Gangetic Plain)

    There is about 300 tigers in this area, most notably in Jim Corbett National Park, Nainital; Rajaji National Park, Haridwar and Pilibhit Tiger Reserve, Rohilkhand

  • Central Indian Highlands

    There is about 550 tigers in this area, most notably in Ranthambore National Park, Rajasthan; Kuno National Park, Gwalior and Bandhavgarh National Park, Umaria.

  • Eastern Ghats

    There is about 50 tigers in this area, Tamil Nadu and Kerala, most notably in Sri Venkateshwara National Park, Tirupati and the largest tiger resrve in India, Nagarjunsagar-Srisailam Tiger Reserve, Andhra Pradesh.

  • Western Ghats

    There is about 450 tigers in the joint areas of the states Karnataka, most notably in Bandipur National Park, Bandipur; Periyar National Park, Idukki and Mudumalai National Park, Nilgiri.

  • North Eastern Hills

    There is about 500 tigers in this area, most notably in Manas National Park, Assam; Namdapha National Park, Arunachal Pradesh and Kaziranga National Park, Assam.

  • Sunderbans Mangrove Delta

    Arguably the most famous Bengal Tiger reserve, this mangrove forest spanning India and Bangladesh holds a population of about 90 tigers.

Bangladesh

In Bangladesh, a large number of Bengal Tigers usually reside in the Indian adjacent Sunderbans Mangrove Delta, with a small amount in the Myanmar-India adjacent Chittagong Hill Tracts. These 114 Bangladeshi tigers are threatened by rising sea levels, habitat loss and poaching, with the Bangladeshi Bengal Tiger population predicted to go extinct by 2070.

Nepal

The tiger population of Nepal is only found in the Terai region. This population is largely split into the three isolated subpopulations, being Chitwan-Parsa National Park, Bardiya National Park and the Shuklaphanta Wildlife Reserve. Although the tiger population was declining, recent efforts increased it to around 350 tigers.

Bhutan

Bengal Tigers in Bhutan usually inhabit the subtropical Himalayan foothills in the south of the country. The major tiger population of about 131 is mainly in the Royal Manas and Jigme Singye Wangchuck National Parks.

China (Tibet)

The Chinese population of Bengal Tigers is virtually extinct, with just a population of 1-10 tigers constantly circling South Tibet and Arunachal Pradesh/Bhutan, with the sightings mainly in the Tibet border province of Mêdog.
